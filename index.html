Hello! I'm Dat-Danny Ta - Tạ Văn Đạt /ta˨˩ˀ. văn˧. ɗat˨˩/ (谢文达), currently a student at Peking University. I am particularly interested in sociophonetics. Drawing on methods from experimental phonetics, sociolinguistics, and statistical analysis, my research investigates patterns of phonetic variation in speech communities and the mechanisms underlying sound change.

Phonological contrasts are often realized through multiple phonetic cues, which differ in their relative cue weighting. Such cue-weighting patterns are reflected in both speech production and speech perception. When the weighting of cues within a phonological contrast shifts—whether in production, perception, or both—this may signal the onset of sound change.

My research therefore focuses on cue weighting and the mapping between production and perception in processes of phonetic and phonological change. My previous work has primarily examined tones and vowels in Northern Vietnamese, investigating how their acoustic properties and perceptual cues contribute to the maintenance and evolution of phonological contrasts.

In addition, I am also interested in second language speech production and perception. My research in this area includes studies on the production and perception of Mandarin tones by native Vietnamese learners, exploring how learners acquire and weight phonetic cues when processing tonal contrasts in a second language.
