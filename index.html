Hello! I'm Danny Van-Dat Ta - Tạ Văn Đạt /ta˨˩ˀ. văn˦. ɗat˧˨/ (谢文达), currently a graduate student at Peking University. I am particularly interested in sociophonetics and second language acquisition. My research primarily employs experimental phonetic methods and statistical analysis, combined with theories from sociolinguistics and historical linguistics, to investigate phonetic variation in society and the mechanisms of sound change.

Phonological contrasts are typically realized through multiple phonetic cues in both production and perception, which vary in their relative weighting. Shifting in the relative weighting of cues within a phonological contrast may signal the initiation of sound change.

My research therefore focuses on cue weighting and the mapping between production and perception in processes of phonetic and phonological change. My previous work has primarily examined tones and vowels in Northern Vietnamese, investigating how their acoustic properties and perceptual cues contribute to the maintenance and evolution of phonological contrasts.

In addition, I am also interested in second language speech production and perception. My research in this area includes studies on the production and perception of Mandarin Chinese tones by native Vietnamese learners, exploring how learners acquire and weight phonetic cues when processing tonal contrasts in a second language.
